Hi Chris,

I will be happy to add you to the list of developers of WSJT.

I have already seen and approved your application to join the wsjt-svn 
mailing list.  This list contains automatically generated notifications 
whenever one of us submits a change to the code database.

You should also subscribe to the wsjt-devel list, which is used for 
internal communications among the developers group.  I will send a copy 
of this message to the wsjt-devel list, by way of introducing you to the 
group.

You will learn that development effort tends to occur in spurts, 
whenever there is a perceived need and one or more of us has some 
programming time available.

There are two active branches in the WSJT repository.  The "trunk" 
contains the WSJT program itself; "branches/MAP65" contains the closely 
related program MAP65, some early descriptions of which can be found at
http://physics.princeton.edu/pulsar/K1JT/MAP65_Status.txt and 
http://physics.princeton.edu/pulsar/K1JT/MAP65_Quick_Start.txt .

In order to be granted developer access (as opposed to anonymous access) 
to the WSJT repository, you must register at Berlios and tell me your 
unix login name there.

Welcome to the group.  I hope you will enjoy working with us.

	-- 73, Joe, K1JT
