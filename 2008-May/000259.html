Almost certainly the reason it's a problem in WSPR, but has 
not been a problem in WSJT or MAP65, is that in WSJT and 
MAP65 the several threads are started just once.  They 
remain running thereafter, using idle loops when they have 
nothing to do.  In WSPR, on the other hand, threads are 
started as needed to do something (i.e., decode a block of 
received data), and they exit when done.
