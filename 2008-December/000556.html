> There appears to be a GUI anomaly when compiling WSPR on Linux. The UTC dB
> DT Freq Drift – Label is nomadic. It is miss positioned and moves depending
> upon Screen Resolution. Probably due to the way Linux deals with the

There are many opportunities for "GUI anomalies" depending 
on options you have chosen (or accepted as defaults) in your 
installed version of Linux.

Geometry of a Python user-interface window depends on things 
selected under system "Look and Feel", on the fonts you're 
using, and probably on other parameters I'm not recalling at 
the moment.

Fonts used by WSPR are controlled by a file "wsprrc".  If 
you don't already have such a file in your WSPR directory, 
copy "wsprrc.win" to "wsprrc" and then edit it as desired. 
Changes (or even making no changes) will most likely require 
edits to lines 943-944 of wspr.py, to make column headings 
line up properly.

> The Label – lab2 is actually outside iframe2, although it appears to be and
> is intended to be inside. 

Hmmm, I don't know what you mean here.  On my Linux screen 
the label is certainly inside iframe2, but it needs to be 
moved slightly to the left (line 944) and the spacing 
between column headings needs tweaking (line 943).

I don't know of any reasonable way to make these things 
fully platform- and font-independent.
