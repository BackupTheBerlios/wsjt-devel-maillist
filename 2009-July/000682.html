Hi Ermanno,

I have spent much more time optimizing MAP65 execution speed 
on Windows than on Linux, so perhaps this makes some 
difference.  In a quick test I just obtained the following 
results for average decoding time on the same 10 *.tf2 files:

Win/XP, 2.8 GHz Pentium 4:  20.7 s
Kubuntu 9.04, 2.4 GHz Xeon: 46.0 s

In these tests a significant portion of the time is for 
reading the 46 MB files from disk.  In normal operation this 
time does not appear, since the data are acquired by network 
transfer during the preceding minute.  I have not tried a 
speed test of MAP65/Windows and MAP65/Linux on the same 
computer.

I have not seen the problem you mention with /dev/ttyS0 , 
but then I do not normally use MAP65 under Linux in my 
shack.  Perhaps there is a bug in this code.

	-- Joe, K1JT
