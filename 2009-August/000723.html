Hello Joe et al.

I am playing with WSPR using the ATS-3b altoids tin transceiver kit.

http://kd1jv.qrpradio.com/ATS3B/ats3b.HTM

I extended the ATS-3b firmware to transmit WSPR in standalone mode. To my great pleasure, the very first evening I switched it on I was spotted at New Zeland. I live in Plainsboro, NJ.

http://www.kufr.cz/~ok1iak/HAM/WSPR/index.php3

ATS-3b and other QRP kits usually have pretty narrow IF bandwidth centered around 600 or 800Hz. Your software expects the SSB IF filter be centered at 2.5kHz. Would you please extend your WSPR software to provide configurable audio frequency center?

I would like to add WSPR to my PocketDigi software, which is a multimode HF modem for Pocket PC platform. Most of my contribution was porting from gMFSK and optimizing for fixed point arithmetics and performance. All the modes run on a modest 206MHz StrongARM. It would be nice to have the WSPR decoder running on PDA or cell phone using little real estate and power.

http://sourceforge.net/projects/pocketdigi/
http://www.n0hr.com/PocketDigi/PocketDigi_intro.htm

I am having difficulties to read the Fortran code. I have the encoder implemented. I know how to decode the message from soft detected four bit symbols - I will just use Phil Karn's code. It would help me a lot, if you could describe in short how your receive algorithm works - how many FFTs at which speed and spacing are you running, how do you lock onto the sync vector and whether you run any AFC and how the AFC is implemented.

If I do succeed, Simon Brown of the Ham Radio Deluxe & DM780 fame will integrate the WSPR port also.

Thanks and 73, Vojtech AB2ZA
