Joe Taylor and Others

The WSJTX release version for Windows works well with my Icom IC703 and IC7200 radios with a SLUSB
external sound card and HP PC and laptop.

The faster JT9 decoding is very helpful
- my PC used to take up to 30s to decode 10 JT9 messages and make it difficult to reply in the same minute.
The longest decoding time now is about 20 seconds.

The "Split" TX mode scheme works with both radios as long as you leave time, about 5 seconds, for a lot of reading 
and writing (seems like more than really necessary) of the A and B frequencies  by the CIV controller
before manualy setting the SPLIT mode or activating the antenna tuner on the radio.
Both  of these actions can cause current CAT commands to be aborted and sometimes leave the A / B VFOs 
incorrectly set.

I have been looking at the source code to see how difficult it would be to compile it myself.
It looks like a lot of effort.
Looking at the sources for the first time, they are an odd mixture of old-style Fortran and the latest GUI language.
Some of the more difficult bits of coding, particularly decoding and error correction, come from
general purpose programs written by KA9Q. He probably deserves a more visible credit somewhere.
