This is useful, and it is certainly not urgent at all (if it is useful).

But I am not sure the text "I=Left Q=Right" for the unchecked state is
correct. Or at least, if it is, then the labels in Rocky are incorrect. For
my softrock 40m receiver I use the setting "Left/Right=Q/I". It is correct.
The PSK band is in the right place and SSB signals are LSB. For the same
radio, I need to uncheck the reversal box in WSPR. I am not sure if you are
right or Rocky, but I don't think that you can both be correct.

Regarding the formula, it is indeed very useful. Even after I figured out
how to get my receiver to work I did not realize that if the BFO is not 1500
Hz then Fiq needs to shift.

But the text above it is not exactly correct, I think. It says "The default
value for Fiq is 12000 Hz; this puts the 200 Hz WSPR band 12 kHz above
mid-band." I think the WSPR band is shifted another 1500 Hz up from the
softrock's mid band.

> consider it a very high priority.  Also: I don't claim to know whether the
> correspondence between stereo channel and 3.5 mm connector is "Left=Ring,
> Right=Tip", or the opposite.
>

I don't know that either. But since WSPR does not show a panoramic spectrum,
it is hard to figure out which setting is right. With Rocky or SDR-Radio it
is easy, so what I think many people will do is correlate their Rocky
setting to the way they need to set up WSPR. In some sense the WSPR setting
is "blind"; you don't get visual feedback if it's wrong.
