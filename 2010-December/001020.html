Hi Sivan,

Like John, I haven't seen the condition you describe... but your 
hypothesis is likely to be correct.  I'm a neophyte at programming 
threads, and quite possibly have done something silly, somewhere.

Supposedly, "critical sections" of Fortran code are enclosed in pairs of 
calls to cs_lock/cs_unlock:

   cs_lock()
   ...
   cs_unlock

For the most part, these have been used to protect Fortran I/O -- 
because those I/O routines are not re-entrant.  Some other things may 
need "cs_" protection, as well.
