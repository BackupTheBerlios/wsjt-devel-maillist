A correction and some progress.

First, I noticed that the 3.0 manual does state which channel is I and which
is Q in the straight and reversed IQ settings. Sorry about this, but I still
think it would still be good to label them as such in the GUI.

Second, I figured out the Fiq formula (Fiq=dial_freq -
softrock_center_freq). For my 40m softrock receiver, whose central frequency
is 7.056, the settings is theoretically -17400. In practice I found out that
-17285 is much closer to the correct setting.

Sivan

On Wed, Dec 22, 2010 at 12:13 PM, Sivan Toledo <stoledo at tau.ac.il> wrote:

> Hi Joe,
>
> Two small suggestions for the 3.0 manual and GUI:
>
> 1. Rather than "Reverse TX IQ" and "Reverse RX IQ" checkboxes, I suggest
> the more informative "I=left, Q=right" and "I=right, Q=left" with radio
> buttons or a combo box. This has two advantages. First, it tells people what
> to do: they can trace the I and Q routing in their radio (almost; the
> softrock schematics don't say which channel is I and which is Q, but this is
> easy to fix), then figure out whether the tip or ring is left, and they are
> done. With "correct" vs. "reverse" terminology there is no way to figure out
> what to do. Second, this is the terminology used in Rocky, and it's easy to
> figure out in Rocky whether you are reversed or not (e.g. by checking that
> the PSK31 bands which are easy to identify are in the right frequency, or by
> checking an SSB signal for correct USB or LSB). I came up with the
> suggestion while reading the manual, but this is better fixed in the GUI
> than just in the manual.
>
> 2. It would be good to add the formula for setting Fiq. I tried to hunt for
> the WSPR band yesterday with a crystal-controlled RX-only softrock, even
> though I knew the center frequency with reasonable accuracy and even though
> I was able to spot the PSK31 signals in Rocky. Between the dial frequency,
> BFO frequency, and Fiq, I could not figure out how to set Fiq. It would be
> good to have a formula, perhaps with an example.
>
> Regards, Sivan
