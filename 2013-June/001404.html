> I am however having operational issues. I am unable to see the complete JT9

> FFT bin/pixel, but this makes the signals look quite narrow and difficult

What's your screen resolution?  On a 1440x900 monitor the display seems 
good to me.  More is better, especially if you want to use the wider 
bandwidths made possible by v1.1.  Less is usable, but certainly less 
desirable.

I don't find tuning difficult at Bins/Pixel = 4 or even 5, and it seems 
to me that both JT65 and JT9 signals are easily dealth with at that 
resolution.  Since the program finds signals automatically, anyway, 
there's not much "tuning" to do, by hand.

The low-frequency limit of displayed spectrum is currently fixed at 
zero.  My thinking was that the filters in most radios go down to 500 Hz 
and maybe lower, so not much space could be saved by starting at a 
higher value.

> I also find confusing and
> distracting to have JT65 signals on the same window as JT9, but is jut my
> personal opinion.

Of course there's no need to have both modes in the displayed window. 
Users with max BW in the 2.4 to 2.7 kHz range, especially, may want to 
concentrate on one mode or the other.

One motivation for making it possible to have both modes available at 
once is that it should help people to see that at most about 10 JT65 
signals can fit into the range displayed by JT65-HF without overlap, 
while more like 120 JT9 signals could do so.
