Hi Joe,

Agreed, it would of course change the mode.

As WSJT-X is quite a major change, it would be the ideal time to make 
changes to the sampling rate.
To prevent confusion, JT4Xg could be a new designator.
(same for JT65X ??)

Question is what are the advantages, if any, of moving to 12000 sampling ??

I see many soundcards do not sample at 11025, and perform a conversion (or 
the driver/windows/Linux does) from 48KHz or above.
Is there a performance hit as a result ?
I imagine many cards do not convert well, expecting ears as an end user, not 
a scientific instrument.

If all modes use the same sample rate (12000) is it easier for one decoder 
to identify the different modes, or multiple modes all at once ?
I have wideband sampling and MAP65 in mind here.

Simplifies dedicated hardware samplers.


Thoughts ??
