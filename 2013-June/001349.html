> ....Adding other WSJT modes: JT65, FSK441, ISCAT, JT4 ...
>
> As you know I have a specific personal interest in JT4.
> When it is added to WSJT-X will the sampling rate change to 12000 from
> 11025 ??
> I can see this will have an impact on the tone spacing if it is changed.
>
> Curious to know how it will be done.

The tone spacing is part of the protocol definition.  It can't be 
changed, and still call the mode "JT4".

When the other modes are added to WSJT-X, they will remain backward 
compatible with earlier versions of WSJT and other programs implementing 
the WSJT modes.

Basic information on compiling the WSJT programs has been available for 
some time at http://physics.princeton.edu/pulsar/K1JT/devel.html .  This 
is not a step-by step recipe -- but if you generally know what you're 
doing with such tasks, it's enough to get you started.

Building these programs is not for the faint of heart.
