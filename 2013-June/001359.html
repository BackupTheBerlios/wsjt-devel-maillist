John --

No objection, of course, but you're playing with code that is intended
to work for me -- not necessarily anyone else, at least without some
instruction.

As I mention before, there are some some hard-wired features that will
probably bite you.

I've set my TS-2000 Rx bandwidth limits at 200 - 5000 Hz, and dial
frequency 14.076.  JT9 signals are then 3000 - 4000 Hz.  But the Tx SSB
filter won't pass audio that high; so I set XIT = 2000 Hz and subtract
2000 Hz from the audio frequency.

You are probably trying to transmit at an audio frequency < 2000 Hz.  So 
after 2000 Hz is subtracted (for the supposed XIT), your tones come out 
apparently "inverted".

	-- Joe
