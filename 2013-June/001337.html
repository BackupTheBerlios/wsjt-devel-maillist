On 05/06/13 07:05, Joe Taylor wrote:
> Hi David,
>
> Thanks for the report.
>
> I know there are some issues with JT9-2 (as there were, also, with the 
> -5, -10, and -30 submodes), but I don't consider it a high priority to 
> work on those problems.
>
> Those submodes were intended for experimentation at MF and LF. Their 
> tone spacings are too small for them to work well with the Doppler 
> spreads and oscillator stabilities typical on the HF bands.  Moreover, 
> the T/R sequences are too slow (read "boring") for most people to find 
> them attractive to use, most of the time.
>
> After all, JT9-2 offers a 3 dB advantage at best.  You can often 
> overcome a 3 dB shortfall simply by turning up the power (say, from 5 
> W to 10 W), or waiting for QSB to bring the signal up by a bit.  Even 
> with that wait, you may finish the QSO sooner than you would with one 
> of the slower submodes.
>
> We have plenty of experience with these issues with JT modes used for 
> EME at VHF and UHF.  T/R sequences longer than one minute have never 
> been popular.  JT65 offers the ability to average several successive 
> transmissions, which works well when another 2-4 dB is needed to make 
> a QSO.  In principle such capability could be programmed for JT9, as 
> well, although I have no plans to do so at present.
>
> Bottom line: unless and until someone offers a persuasive argument 
> that JT9-2 (or a slower submode) has an important application and 
> would see significant use, I'm not planning to spend more time on it.
>
>     -- 73, Joe, K1JT
