> Hi David,
>
>> As WSJT-X is quite a major change, it would be the ideal time to make
>> changes to the sampling rate.
>> To prevent confusion, JT4Xg could be a new designator.
>> (same for JT65X ??)
>
> There's no need to change the mode or have a new designator.  JT65A is 
> already incorporated into WSJT-X.  The program's sample rate is 12000 Hz 
> on input, and in JT65 mode the data are re-sampled to 11025 Hz inside 
> WSJT-X using a high-quality conversion algorithm that loses no 
> sensitivity.
>
> The output sample rate is 48000 Hz, and the Tx waveform is generated 
> on-the-fly during each transmission.
>
>> Question is what are the advantages, if any, of moving to 12000 sampling 
>> ??
>
> Most recent sound cards and motherboard sound systems use a 48000 Hz 
> native sample rate.  Conversion to 12000 Hz is easy, but conversion to 
> 11025 Hz (done in the driver routines) often cuts corners so that the 
> final sample rate is off by around 0.7% -- a big error, by WSJT standards.
>
>> I see many soundcards do not sample at 11025, and perform a conversion
>> (or the driver/windows/Linux does) from 48KHz or above.
>> Is there a performance hit as a result ?
>
> Yes, if lousy resampling algorithms are used.
>
>> If all modes use the same sample rate (12000) is it easier for one
>> decoder to identify the different modes, or multiple modes all at once ?
>> I have wideband sampling and MAP65 in mind here.
>
> Again: no need to change the modes.  They are fundamentally defined in 
> terms of frequencies and tone durations, independent of sample rates.
>
> -- Joe, K1JT
