Hi Joe,

I used fmin/fmax to restrict the range thinking that I was helping the decoder to "concentrate" on the region I wanted.  But after you have looked at the possible decoder penalties and if these are minimal, then it seems setting fmin/fmax is not particularly relevant.   Useful as a guide to the two ranges, perhaps.

I wrote a simple shell script:   setxit 200   or setxit 0

#!/bin/sh
rigctl -m 210 -r /dev/tty.usbserial -s 4800 Z $1

which I use remotely in a separate terminal window to make the necessary XIT change.    If something like this could be incorporated automatically linked to the JT65 or JT9 selection, that would be very convenient.

Thanks for the noise explanation.  I had noticed that JT65 SNR seemed not to compare with JT9 SNR reports and wondered why.     Your suggestion about an "Allow expanded reports" option is probably best.

A different point:  I notice that my (G4KLA) JT9 SNR recvd reports on the PSK website are all  +0, and not reported for JT65.  A problem for Edson, perhaps.
