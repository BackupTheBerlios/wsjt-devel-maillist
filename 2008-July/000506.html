Hullo Bruce,

I meant to be more specific - the WSJT window can move and not affect  
the coordinates.  However,   do this in routine set_freezedf in  
specjt.py

     print root.geometry()

and then move the SPECJT window and then click in the waterfall area  
which activates this routine.   You will find that root.geometry()  
has the complete coordinates (width,height,x,y) of the WSJT  
window.    SpecJTGeometry in WSJT.INI is then updated with the x,y  
position of the WSJT.

I agree that if you put the SPECJT window back where it was before  
you moved it, then the x-coordinate is correct.  My statement was  
slightly misleading.   The main problem is why root.geometry is  
linked to the WSJT window.   Since it works correctly when specjt.py  
is launched on its own, there appears to be some dependence on the  
way specjt is launched from within wsjt.py which causes confusion  
between Tkinter and the Mac window manager.

I have tried a variety of calls to get at coordinates (using more  
than one manual on Tkinter and python) but the results are always the  
same:  wrong!
