Bill,

> Thinking about this some more, there's no reason why the symlink itself has to be in /dev so I'm not giving up on that approach either.

I am trying to think of the user who doesn't want to know what is going on behind the scenes and has expertise which does not extend much beyond using a text editor.  Clearly we can accept that users who are (more or less) Unix compliant can cope with the concepts of symlinks and beyond.

But just as clearly there are users who want to come to the K1JT download page, download a program and press start.     I do think we need to cater for these folks.   I do not think we should see symlinks as a a useful solution except in those circumstances where there is simply no other way to solve the problem of user extensions.   We are not there yet.
