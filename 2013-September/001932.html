Hi John and all,

John Nelson wrote:
  > fil4 is a marked improvement over fil1 (borrowed from WSPR) as 
judged by the waveform on the waterfall display.

Yes, it would be.  Subroutine fil1() from WSPR was designed to process 
an entire reception interval in one go, so there were no "edge effects" 
between many small sub-blocks of data.

When you tried fil1 in WSJT-X, you made the number of 12000 Hz samples 
appear to come out right by forcing n2=n1/4.  But the actual "n2" 
returned from fil1 was less than n1/4.  The difference in these numbers 
(as I recall, it would have been 39 samples) would be the number of 
undefined (or badly defined) samples added to each block.  Not good!

The other problem with fil1() is that its low-pass cutoff was set at 3 
kHz.  In WSJT-X we want more like 4.5 kHz.

	-- Joe, K1JT
