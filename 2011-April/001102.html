> Looks like it may be possible to us a still narrower filter (in this
> unscattered, non doppler shifted mode...).

Sorry, no.

Not because of any programming issue; it's just basic physics.  The JT4A 
mode (and the "A" filter in any of the other JT4 sub-modes) uses channel 
bandwidth equal to the keying rate, 4.375 Hz.  This means the switched 
tones are actually that wide, and any narrower filters would remove 
signal as well as noise.
