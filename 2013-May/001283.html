On Sun, May 12, 2013 at 4:37 PM, Greg Beam <ki7mt at yahoo.com> wrote:

> Hi Dave,
>
> I don't have a Mac in the shack :-) but the the XYL does development on a
> MacBook Pro with the latest SDK, Qt, etc etc with OS, 10.7. I did some
> poking around on to see where things were located.
>
> Couple Things:
>
> First, for the the Makfile.linux (make -f Makefile.linux) you need to edit
> the INCPATH:
>
> INCPATH =  -I'/usr/include/qt4' -I'/usr/include/qt4/QtCore'
>
> For me, Qwt-qt4 Libs were installed here:
>
> INCPATH =  -I'/lib/qt4-mac/include/qwt -I'/lib/qt4-mac/include/qwt/
> QtCore'
>
> You'll need to check on your box where the libs are located and adjust
> accordingly.
>
> Same thing for wsjtx.pro:
>
> Adjust the qwt/include and LIBS+= statement according to your system:
>
> unix {
> INCLUDEPATH += $$quote(/lib/qt4-mac/include/qwt)
> LIBS += ../wsjtx/lib/libjt9.a
> LIBS += -lhamlib
> LIBS += -lportaudio -lgfortran -lfftw3f -lqwt
> }
>
> echo $PATH showed the /libs dir in the path, so you could try excluding
> the INCLUDEPATH directive. If you know where they are, probably best to
> include it.
>
> One last thing. When you run make -f Makefile.linux the first time, you
> need to ensure the install directory wsjt_install actually exists,
> otherwise, jt9 will be copied to "wsjt_install" as a file, v.s. being
> placed in the directory of ../wsjt_install. It should be at the root level
> of the svn co ../wsjtx and ../wsjtx_install
>
> Good Luck,
