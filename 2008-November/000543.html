> The following are suggestions for future improvements
> 
> 1- Personaly I would like to have a "sked" mode (50% transmit rate, with 
> the choice of the period 1st/2nd). That would allow to better analyze 
> short openings, such as 80/160 antipodal grayline openings...

Sounds like a reasonable idea.  I'll think about it for a future release.

> 2- I still feel that some soundcard "sample rate" correction should be 
> made possible to get the optimum decoding capability. This feature 
> exists on digital terminals such as mixW and MMTTY. On a few digi-modes 
> like Olivia, a careful adjustement makes a big difference (but even on 
> PSK31 and TTY it makes a diffrence on signals near the decoding threshold).
> The a/m software use a visual method (the SSTV terminal for mixW). You 
> just tune on a "time signal" and you get the lines produced by the time 
> signal as vertical as possible...  once the lines are perfectly 
> vertical, you just press record and that's it...
> Actualy WSPR does not need to include such visual terminal, but just a 
> way of setting the "sample rate correction" once measured with another 
> software.

My experience is that WSJT needs sample-rate correction on some 
computers because their sound hardware always samples at 48 kHz, and the 
software driver does a poor job of resampling to yield the 11025 Hz 
samples required by WSJT.  To get around this problem, WSPR asks for 
samples at 12000 Hz.  I have yet to see a computer that produces 
significant (say, 0.1% or larger) sample-rate errors when the requested 
rate is 12000 Hz.  If you (or anyone else) can document otherwise, I 
would like to hear more about it.

> 3- I feel that many WSPR users are overdriving their soundcard input, 
> which generates "false decodes"  . I think than a  "optimum audio 
> level"  indicator should  be made available  within WSPR software  
> (something similar  to the WSJT audio indicator)

Obviously this would be a nice feature.  It has been on my "to do" list, 
  and still is.  In the meantime, just start up WSJT, hit "Monitor", and 
set the audio level level to "0 dB" there.
