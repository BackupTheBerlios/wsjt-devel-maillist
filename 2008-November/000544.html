Hi Joe,
re sampling rate-correction, a little addendum:
Something I noticed, is that feeding the same RX audio to 2 PC's I could 
see differences
I made a post about it, some time ago on the WSPRnet forum 
http://wsprnet.org/drupal/node/139
for example:
PC1: 080924 1018 16 -19 1.5 10.140110 OK1DST JN79 33 0 2 0
PC2: 080924 1018 11 -23 1.4 10.140110 OK1DST JN79 33 0 1 0
PC1 080924 1032 29 -4 1.1 10.140147 EB1APK IN73 40 0 1 0
PC2 080924 1032 24 -8 1.1 10.140147 EB1APK IN73 40 0 1 0

The PC1 uses a microham USB-sound device, while the PC2 is an IBM 
"thinkpad" laptop
Last but not least: As already mentionned, I cannot get WSPR working on 
my desktop soundcard (while I' ve been using WSJT on it successfully at 
the beginning of JT6 )

Another point I forgot to mention:
The WSPR displayed DT, seems to have 1s offset:
- using my 2 stations sync on the same clock through dimension4 I see a 
constant DT+1 (or 0.9 to 1.1)
- I see the same DT +1 on a large majority of stations
I see the same, when time synchronized manually using a DCF77 clock or a 
GPS (when I don't have Internet access)
When I operate JT65, I see DT 0, most of the time...
But I am sure you  noticed it...

>> The following are suggestions for future improvements
>>
>> 1- Personaly I would like to have a "sked" mode (50% transmit rate, 
>> with the choice of the period 1st/2nd). That would allow to better 
>> analyze short openings, such as 80/160 antipodal grayline openings...
>
>
> Sounds like a reasonable idea.  I'll think about it for a future release.
>
>> 2- I still feel that some soundcard "sample rate" correction should 
>> be made possible to get the optimum decoding capability. This feature 
>> exists on digital terminals such as mixW and MMTTY. On a few 
>> digi-modes like Olivia, a careful adjustement makes a big difference 
>> (but even on PSK31 and TTY it makes a diffrence on signals near the 
>> decoding threshold).
>> The a/m software use a visual method (the SSTV terminal for mixW). 
>> You just tune on a "time signal" and you get the lines produced by 
>> the time signal as vertical as possible...  once the lines are 
>> perfectly vertical, you just press record and that's it...
>> Actualy WSPR does not need to include such visual terminal, but just 
>> a way of setting the "sample rate correction" once measured with 
>> another software.
>
>
> My experience is that WSJT needs sample-rate correction on some 
> computers because their sound hardware always samples at 48 kHz, and 
> the software driver does a poor job of resampling to yield the 11025 
> Hz samples required by WSJT.  To get around this problem, WSPR asks 
> for samples at 12000 Hz.  I have yet to see a computer that produces 
> significant (say, 0.1% or larger) sample-rate errors when the 
> requested rate is 12000 Hz.  If you (or anyone else) can document 
> otherwise, I would like to hear more about it.
>
>> 3- I feel that many WSPR users are overdriving their soundcard input, 
>> which generates "false decodes"  . I think than a  "optimum audio 
>> level"  indicator should  be made available  within WSPR software  
>> (something similar  to the WSJT audio indicator)
>
>
> Obviously this would be a nice feature.  It has been on my "to do" 
> list,  and still is.  In the meantime, just start up WSJT, hit 
> "Monitor", and set the audio level level to "0 dB" there.
>
