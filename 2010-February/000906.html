> Now, if I shift modes to say JT65, I then see the following in the
> shell.  It might be worth noting that when I start in FSK144 or JT6M,
> the default SpecJT view is the horizontal H1 view.  Once I shift to any
> of these other modes, the SpecJT view changes to "speed: 3" vertical
> view.  It continues to work and I can shift back to the H1 view.

This is correct behavior.  FSK441 and JT6M are best viewed with the 
fast-moving, low-frequency-resolution, horizontally scrolling 
waterfalls; JT65 is best viewed with a slow, high-resolution, vertically 
scrolling waterfall.
