I'm sure that it is NOT a matter of different g95/gfortran calling  
conventions.   Otherwise all the fortran routines which call C  
routines (eg.  th_wspr2 in wspr1.F90 and th_decode in startdec.F90)  
would have to have #ifdefs all over the place with th_wspr2_ and  
th_decode_  etc for Windows.....    That they do not tell me that  
this is not a difference in fortran calling conventions, but  
something peculiar in the way the fftw library has been constructed  
which requires this special treatment.

The fftw library I construct for Macs of all sorts, follows  
conventions and up to now, I have had to modify four2a.f to remove  
the _ to retain standard conventions.

Your #ifdef fix will get round the non-standard fftw library  
construction in a simple way.    Presumably there is something wrong  
with the configure step in the fftw package that causes this problem  
with some OSs...
