Hi Pierre,

The important point is the last few lines:

mkdir -p build/lib/WsprMod
mv w.so WsprMod/w.so
pierre at pierre-desktop:~/wspr$

This tells me that you have successfully compiled the program.   Have  
you tried to execute:   python wspr.py?    If it fails, what messages  
do you get?

The "error" messages are really warnings:

 > rmbadname1: Replacing "len" with "len_bn".

These are standard fixes by f2py where it prefers that Fortran code  
should not  use key words in argument lists that f2py uses.   Not an  
error.

 > analyzeline: Failed to evaluate '512)             !length of fft'.

f2py does not like comments   such as  " !length of fft "   to be on  
variable definition lines such as real or integer; and also on any  
lines which call a subroutine.   Again, a warning and not an  
error.    f2py is not really uptodate with the way that comments can  
be added in-line in Fortran code.   A pity...

 > buildmodule: Could not found the body of interfaced routine "gran".

Also, not an error.   This is just that these routines are not part  
of the f2py translation.   Ignore.  (f2py is fairly talkative and  
hence can be confusing....)
