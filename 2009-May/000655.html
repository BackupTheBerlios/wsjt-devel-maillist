> Hi Dick,

> I am not familiar with fldigi having only come across W1HKJ software
> as a result of your email which Joe forwarded to me.
>
> WSJT software incorporates everything you need by way of waterfall
> displays and spectrum analysis.     You only need a mechanism to
> connect the USB (sound output) from your Mac to a suitable interface
> to your rig.   I use the little SignaLink box (USB cable to a Mac,
> either PowerBook or Intel iMac) and a cable from the SignaLink output
> port to my rig.   I use a TS870 (for WSPR) or TS790 (2m WSJT for
> EME).  It seems to me that you don't need to interface WSJT to fldigi
> to get it to work.
>
> I have not yet worked up a full binary download for Macs.
> Therefore you would need to build WSJT yourself.    You will require
> some essential infrastructure starting with the (free) XCode
> Developer kit from Apple which provides compilers.
>
> You would need to get the WSJT source software, configure and
> compile.   Visit this URL:
>
> http://developer.berlios.de/svn/?group_id=5666
>
> for instructions on how to get the full software, then
>
> a)      configure --with-portaudio-lib-dir=/usr/local/lib --with-
> portaudio-include-dir=/usr/local/include
> b)      make
>
> There is also some infrastructure that you need that can be obtained
> from open software sources.     Let me know what Mac you have, and
> whether you have XCode installed and whether you have the python
> infrastructure in place - and then we can go from there.
>
